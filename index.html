1. Overview

Tom Yum Kung is a hot and sour soup considered one of the most iconic dishes in Thai cuisine. "Tom" means boiling, and "Yum" refers to spicy and sour flavors, while "Kung" stands for shrimp. It is known for its zesty broth, which is infused with lemongrass, kaffir lime leaves, galangal, and chilies, giving the soup a refreshing and aromatic taste.

2. History

Tom Yum Kung has its roots in central Thailand, where fresh water and seafood are abundant. Over time, it evolved into a national favorite and became popular across the world as Thai cuisine gained recognition internationally. The rich combination of fresh herbs and spices gives Tom Yum its unique flavor that distinguishes it from other soups.

3. Ingredients

  • 500g large shrimp (peeled and deveined)
  • 4 cups water or chicken broth
  • 2 stalks lemongrass (cut into 2-inch pieces, smashed)
  • 5 kaffir lime leaves (torn)
  • 1-2 Thai chilies (sliced)
  • 3-4 slices galangal
  • 200g mushrooms (sliced)
  • 2-3 tablespoons fish sauce
  • 1-2 tablespoons lime juice
  • 1 tablespoon chili paste (optional)
  • Cilantro leaves (for garnish)

4. Recipe

  1. Bring water or chicken broth to a boil in a large pot.
  2. Add lemongrass, kaffir lime leaves, chilies, and galangal, and let them infuse for 5-10 minutes.
  3. Add mushrooms and cook until tender.
  4. Add shrimp and cook for 2-3 minutes until pink and cooked through.
  5. Season with fish sauce, lime juice, and optional chili paste to taste.
  6. Serve hot and garnish with cilantro leaves.

5. Cultural Significance

Tom Yum Kung represents the perfect balance of flavors in Thai cuisine—spicy, sour, salty, and slightly sweet. It’s commonly served in family meals, restaurants, and even high-end dining venues. The dish is more than just food; it’s a symbol of Thai culinary art and hospitality.

6. Variations

  • Tom Yum Nam Sai: A clear version of the soup without chili paste.
  • Tom Yum Nam Khon: A creamy version made with evaporated or coconut milk.
  • Tom Yum Kung Mae Nam: Uses large freshwater prawns, imparting a unique flavor.
